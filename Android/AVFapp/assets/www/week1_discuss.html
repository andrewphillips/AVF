:: Research - Week 1 Discussion ::

What types of native features (on Android and iOS devices) would you be able to implement with the use of HTML/CSS/JS only?
For both iOS and Android, it appears that I will be able to access the camera, contacts, accelerometer, file, geolocation, local storage, network, media, and notifications.
What types of features do you think will pair well together in an app?
I think, for instance, that if you were accessing the camera, you'd probably want access to file, geolocation, media, and notifications. Honestly, if you pick one element that can be accessed, there's probably some way to combine most if not all the elements into an app, and there are lots of reasons as to why you'd justify putting all the elements into said app. I think that if you were to combine them all, maybe some elements would be more important, and some would be 'in the background'. What I mean is, if you had a camera app, you might want direct access to geolocation and media, but alerts would be something that came as you interfaced with other elements, and not directly seen until needed.
Please describe any design intentions you may have for the AVF Demo app and the revised/new version of your CRUD app.
I would like to work on further customizing my CSS for the AVF app, and make sure that it is catchy/pleasing to the eye. I think that it's currently in more of a placeholder state, and I definitely want to transition out of that and into something that I consider 'more exciting'. I imported my CRUD app from VFW, and I want to make it look more like what I worked on last month in ASD - I had more of a travel themed app, in which you could add media, etc. I don't know if I can get all that functionality into my CRUD app, or if I'll make separate pages for those elements. I want to try to add media access and camera access to my CRUD app if possible.
Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc.
The biggest thing that I've learned is that I have to start early and dig in until it's done. If I try to have a marathon session in the beginning, I find that I can make great lengths in getting my project done, as opposed to having that last-minute-cram-a-thon. I may still need a few days to complete everything, but it's SUCH A BETTER IDEA to go 110% at the start, instead of waiting. I have found that I can pull at least one all-nighter and get so much more accomplished than if I do small segments in the afternoon. I think it's wise to try different methods, and find your rhythm. If you work better in the morning, make that time sacred; if better at night, make that your routine as well. Don't deviate from what works best for you, if you can help it.
